About

Jamie Grefe was raised in a rural Michigan community. He grew up creating noise cassettes, directing gonzo SOV horror comedies, and playing bass guitar in local punk and metal bands. Grefe earned his B.A. in Liberal Studies from Grand Valley State University where he continued to write and direct short films, write, and study the Japanese language. In 2003, he studied Japanese at a small language institute in South Korea while teaching English. He spent the next six years in Japan. Grefe taught, consulted, mixed, edited, and performed noise music under the moniker "Lapdancer", as well as worked professionally as an Assistant Director for live television. Grefe married and moved to Beijing where he taught at the prestigious Tsinghua International School Attached to Tsinghua University. Upon returning to the United States, he earned his M.F.A. in Creative Writing from New England College, spent two years teaching in Taos, New Mexico before settling in Southern California where he currently resides.
