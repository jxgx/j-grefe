About

Jamie Grefe was raised in a rural Michigan community. He grew up creating noise cassettes, directing gonzo SOV horror comedies, and playing bass in local punk and metal bands. Grefe earned his B.A. in Liberal Studies from Grand Valley State University where he continued to direct short films (Meat Salad, Tears for Tono, Will to Flower), research Sartorial Phenomenology, and learned the Japanese language. In 2003, he continued studying Japanese at a small language institute in South Korea while teaching English to young adults. Along with Johnny Unicorn, he recorded the album "Of Whom" by Friend (Furendo). He spent the next six years in Japan. Grefe taught at public and private institutions, translated academic works, and mixed, edited, and performed harsh noise under the moniker "Lap Dancer" (or "Lapdancer"). He also worked professionally as an Assistant Director, (sometimes) Actor, and Editor for live television. Grefe moved to Beijing in 2010 where he led classes at the prestigious Tsinghua International School Attached to Tsinghua University. Upon returning to the United States, he earned his M.F.A. in Creative Writing from New England College under the mentorship of Katie Farris, Matt Bell, and Lily Hoang. He spent two years in Taos, New Mexico before settling in the Los Angeles area where he currently resides.
